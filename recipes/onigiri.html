Onigiri

Description

Onigiri is a popular and versatile Japanese dish known for its simplicity, deliciousness, and portability. Onigiri are rice balls typically formed into triangular or oval shapes and often wrapped in nori (seaweed).

Ingredients

  • Short-grain Japanese rice
  • Water
  • Salt
  • Nori (seaweed)

Steps

  1. Prepare the Rice: Rinse short-grain Japanese rice thoroughly until the water runs clear, then cook it until tender and sticky either in a rice cooker or on the stovetop.
  2. Season the Rice: While the rice is still warm, mix in salt to taste to ensure even distribution throughout the rice.
  3. Shape the Onigiri: Wet your hands lightly with water, take a handful of seasoned rice, and shape it into a triangle, oval, or round ball, optionally creating an indentation for fillings.
  4. Wrap with Nori: Wrap a strip of nori around the onigiri to add flavor and make it easier to handle.
  5. Serve or Store: Enjoy onigiri immediately while warm or at room temperature, or wrap individually in plastic wrap for later consumption.
  6. Enjoy: Onigiri can be eaten as a snack, part of a meal, or packed for picnics and outings, offering a versatile and portable option in Japanese cuisine
